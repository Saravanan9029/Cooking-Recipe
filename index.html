PROCEDURE

1. Heat the biryani vessel and add 200 ml oil and 100 ml ghee.

2. Once the oil is hot, add all whole spices.

3. Add 400 g sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

4. Then, add 100 g ginger paste and 100 g garlic paste.

5. Cook until the raw smell disappears.

6. Now, add 3 green chilies.

7. Sauté for a few minutes and add 2 tablespoons red chilli powder.

8. Mix well until the raw smell disappears.

9. Add 200 ml curd and mix well.

10. Add 400 g chopped tomatoes.

11. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and oil starts separating.

12. Now, add 2 tablespoons salt and mix thoroughly.

13. Add 1 litre 300 ml water and let it boil in High Flame for 10 mins.

14. After 10 Minutes, lower the Flame and let it boil for 5 mins.

15. Now, add the chicken pieces. Cook the chicken for 5 minutes.

16. When the water starts boiling strongly, add the soaked rice.

17. Cook until the rice becomes same level with masala.

18. Stir gently so the rice mixes evenly with the masala without breaking the rice grains.

19. Finally add coriander and mint leaves & Close the Lid.

20. If its Gas Stove, move the biriyani vessel to a pre-heated Dosa Tava and keep it lower flame (Sim) – Let it Dum for 15 mins.

21. If its Wood Burning, Remove the burning Woods from the stove / take some burnt charcoal from the stove and put it over the Lid. Place a Brick to Give pressure on the Lid. Let it Dum for 15 mins.

22. After 15 mins – Open the Lid & Add the remaining Ghee. After 2 Mins - Break the Dum.
